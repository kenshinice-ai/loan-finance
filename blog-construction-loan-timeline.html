Construction Loan Timeline: From Approval to Keys

Understanding progress payments, variation claims, and the common delays that push construction settlements past their dates.

← Back to all articles
ConstructionApril 20, 20269 min read

Building a home is exciting, but construction finance works differently from a standard purchase loan. Understanding the timeline and payment structure helps you plan for costs, delays, and the handover process.

The Construction Loan Process

Unlike a purchase loan where funds are released at settlement, construction loans release money in stages (progress payments) as the builder completes each phase of the build. This means you need to plan for multiple disbursements over several months.

Typical Progress Payment Stages

  1. Deposit: Paid when the construction contract is signed (usually 5–10% of build cost).
  2. Down payment / Slab: Released once the foundation slab is poured and inspected.
  3. Framing: Released after walls, floors, and roof structure are complete.
  4. Lock-up: Released when external walls, windows, and doors are installed.
  5. Fit-off / Closing in: Released when plumbing, electrical, and insulation are complete.
  6. Completion: Final payment after practical completion certificate is issued.

Timeline: What to Expect

  • Loan approval: 1–3 weeks (construction loans take longer than purchase loans due to plan and cost assessment).
  • Construction start: 4–8 weeks after approval, depending on builder scheduling.
  • Average build time: 6–12 months for a standard new home.
  • Final inspection and handover: 2–4 weeks after practical completion.

Common Delays

Construction timelines rarely run perfectly. The most common delays include:

  • Weather: Heavy rain or extreme heat can pause earthworks and concrete pours.
  • Supply chain: Delays in materials such as windows, doors, or fixtures push back stages.
  • Variation claims: Changes requested during construction require lender approval before additional funds are released.
  • Inspection delays: Waiting for the builder's certifier or the lender's valuer to inspect and approve each stage.

Tips for a Smoother Build

  1. Build in buffer time — plan for 10–15% longer than the builder's estimate.
  2. Keep communication open with your builder and adviser throughout the build.
  3. Get written quotes for any variations before they're approved.
  4. Arrange temporary accommodation or rental if your timeline is tight.
