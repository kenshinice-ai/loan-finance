Refinance or Stay Put? When Switching Lenders Makes Sense

A practical framework for comparing your current loan against market options, including exit costs and rate differentials.

← Back to all articles
RefinanceJune 8, 20266 min read

Refinancing your home loan can save thousands over the life of your mortgage — but it's not always the right move. The decision depends on your current rate, remaining loan term, exit costs, and how long you plan to stay in the property.

The Refinance Math

A lower interest rate sounds appealing, but you need to factor in application fees, valuation costs, discharge fees from your current lender, and any break costs if you're still in a fixed-rate period. On a $500,000 loan, even a 0.5% rate reduction can save around $2,500 per year in interest — but only if the switch pays back its upfront costs.

Break Costs Explained

If you're in a fixed-rate term and refinance early, your current lender may charge break costs. These are calculated based on the difference between your fixed rate and the lender's current variable rate for the remaining term. In rising rate environments, break costs can be significant.

When Refinancing Makes Sense

  • Your current rate is above market average by 0.5% or more
  • You've built enough equity to reduce or eliminate LMI
  • Your financial situation has improved (higher income, lower debts)
  • You want to switch from an interest-only to a principal-and-interest loan

When Staying Put Is Better

  • Your fixed rate is still competitive and you have 12+ months remaining
  • Break costs exceed the potential savings over your remaining term
  • You're close to paying off the loan and refinancing adds unnecessary cost

The Rate Negotiation Alternative

Before switching lenders, consider asking your current lender for a rate hold or reduction. Many banks will match competitive offers to retain good customers — often at zero cost.

Our Approach

We run the numbers for every client: comparing total costs over 3–5 years, not just monthly repayments. If refinancing makes sense, we handle the entire process. If staying put is better, you'll know exactly why.
