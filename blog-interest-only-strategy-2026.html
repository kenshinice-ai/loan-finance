Interest-Only Loans in 2026: Still Worth It?

How lenders are tightening IO policies and what investors should consider before locking in a three-to-five-year interest-only period.

← Back to all articles
InvestmentMay 3, 20265 min read

Interest-only (IO) lending has been a cornerstone of Australian investment property strategy for decades. But in 2026, lenders are tightening criteria and some have reduced their IO exposure. Understanding the current landscape is essential before committing to an IO period.

Why Investors Choose Interest-Only

  • Cash flow management: Lower repayments during the IO period free up capital for other investments or business operations.
  • Tax efficiency: IO interest is generally fully tax-deductible against rental income.
  • Capital deployment: Savings from lower repayments can be invested elsewhere at a higher return.

How Lenders Have Changed

Several major banks have reduced their maximum IO period from 10 years to 5–7 years. Some lenders now require a minimum loan-to-value ratio (LVR) of 60% for IO loans, up from the previous 80%. Private lenders remain more flexible but charge higher rates.

The 5-Year Standard

Most mainstream lenders now offer IO periods of 1–5 years. A 5-year IO period is common for new investment purchases, with the expectation that principal repayments will begin once the term ends.

When IO Makes Sense

  1. You have a clear exit strategy (sell, refinance to P&I, or boost contributions)
  2. Your property is in a high-growth corridor where capital appreciation offsets the IO cost
  3. You can invest the repayment difference at a higher net return

When IO May Not Be Ideal

  • The property is in a stagnant or declining market
  • Your LVR is above 70% (IO rates are typically higher)
  • You're nearing retirement and need principal reduction to manage debt

Refinancing at IO Expiry

The most common risk is being unable to refinance from IO to P&I when the period ends. Lenders reassess serviceability, property value, and your financial position. Keeping a strong credit file and maintaining equity buffers reduces this risk.

Our Approach

We model each investor's situation across multiple scenarios — IO for 5 years then P&I, straight P&I from day one, or a hybrid approach. The right strategy depends on your cash flow, tax position, and growth timeline.
