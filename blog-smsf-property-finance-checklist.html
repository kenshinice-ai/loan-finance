SMSF Property Finance: A Step-by-Step Checklist

From trust deed setup to settlement — the documents, limits, and lender requirements you need before your SMSF buys property.

← Back to all articles
SMSFMay 28, 202610 min read

Self-managed super funds can borrow to invest in property, but the structure is more complex than a standard home loan. Understanding the requirements upfront saves time and avoids costly delays at settlement.

The Bare Trust Structure

SMSF property finance requires a separate bare trust (sometimes called a holding trust) to hold the legal title while the property is mortgaged. The lender lends to the trustee of the SMSF, but the bare trust holds the title until the loan is repaid.

Checklist: Before You Apply

  1. SMSF established and active: Your fund should be registered with the ATO and have an executed trust deed.
  2. Bare trust set up: A separate bare trust needs to be established with its own ABN.
  3. Audit history: Most lenders require at least one completed SMSF audit.
  4. Contribution capacity: Clear evidence of ongoing contribution ability to service the loan.
  5. Property type approved: Lenders have specific criteria — residential is widely accepted, commercial varies by lender.

Lending Criteria You'll Face

SMSF lending is a niche market. Most lenders cap loans at 70–80% of the property value, require the property to be solely for investment (no family use), and restrict certain property types such as vacant land or units in buildings with more than four stories.

Single Acquirable Asset Rule

The SMSF can only acquire one property per trust structure. If you want to buy multiple properties, each requires a separate bare trust and loan.

Repayment Strategies

  • Interest-only: Most common during the accumulation phase; keeps repayments lower while the fund grows.
  • Principal and interest: Suitable if contribution capacity is strong.
  • Lump sum contributions: Extra contributions can be used to reduce principal at any time.

Settlement Timeline

SMSF loans typically take 3–5 weeks from application to settlement, slightly longer than standard home loans due to the additional trust documentation. Working with an adviser who specialises in SMSF lending can streamline the process significantly.
